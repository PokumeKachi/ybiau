# Expo - Root - Log

## Theory

### Definition

- First we lay out the foundation of _exponentiation_.
    - Let $a = b^c$

        This means $a$ is equal to $b$ to the power of $c$, as in: By multiplying $1$ by $b$ $c$ times, we get $a$.

    - Examples:

        $2^0=1$, we multiply $0$ times, leaving only 1.

        $3^1=3$, we multiply $1$ times, getting 3.

- Then, deriving from the same definition, $b$ and $c$ shall become the base of _root_ and _logarithm_ respectively.
    - $b=\sqrt[c]{a}$
    - $c=log_ba$

### Formulation

#### Expo

- $b^{c_1}b^{c_2}=b^{c_1+c_2}$

- $\frac{b^{c_1}}{b^{c_2}}=b^{c_1-c_2}$

- $(b^{c_1})^{c_2}=b^{c_1c_2}$

- $(b_1b_2)^c={b_1}^c{b_2}^c$

- $(\frac{b_1}{b_2})^c=\frac{{b_1}^c}{{b_2}^c}$

- $b^{-c}=\frac{1}{b^c}$


#### Root

- $\sqrt[c]{a}=a^{1/c}$

- Most of the time you can just turn roots into fractional exponents

#### Log

- $log_b(a_1a_2)=log_b(a_1)+log_b(a_2)$

- $log_b(\frac{a_1}{a_2})=log_b(a_1)-log_b(a_2)$

- $log_b(a^k)=k\cdot log_b(a)$

- $\frac{log_ba_1}{log_ba_2}=log_{a_2}a_1$
